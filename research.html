Research Focus

My work bridges geochronology, geochemistry, and climate to reconstruct how reef systems and sea level have changed through time. I pair meticulous laboratory methods with transparent, reproducible computation.

  • Coral Paleoclimate Archives Seasonal to multi-decadal reconstructions from coral trace elements and isotopes (LA-ICP-MS, LIBS, RAMAN) to quantify environmental variability.
  • U–Th Geochronology High-precision dating of carbonates, including meteoric calcite phases in altered fossil corals, used to refine Holocene sea-level histories.
  • Method Development Instrument optimization, standards/QC, REE/trace-element workflows at ultra-low levels, and Python-based data reduction and time-series analysis.

Current Projects

Sea Level from Meteoric Calcite

U–Th dating of meteoric calcite in highly altered fossil corals; screening and micro-sampling protocols to improve sea-level reconstructions.

ENSO from Coral Proxies

LA-ICP-MS Mg/Ca and Li/Mg time series; two-stage smoothing and seasonal removal for anomaly detection and event characterization.

Reef Carbonate Chemistry

High-temporal DIC/TA and δ13CDIC on a reef flat; linking metabolic fingerprints to seasonal forcing.
