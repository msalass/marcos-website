Volunteer Teaching in Bangladesh

January–February 2025 — Invited by the Be Well Institute (rural Bangladesh), I designed and taught an undergraduate short course on soil science and biogeochemical nutrient cycles for ~25 students, tailored to local agriculture. We combined lectures with hands-on monitoring of compost and soils (temperature, pH, moisture, respiration, major-element ratios) to connect nutrient cycling and mineral micronutrients with plant health.

  • Hands-on methods: field sampling, lab safety, simple calibration & QA checks
  • Data skills: tidy data, plots, seasonal cycles, basic anomaly detection
  • Applications: compost monitoring, soil quality, nutrient cycling for local crops
