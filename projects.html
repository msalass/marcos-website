Projects

Two current lines of work—kept short and readable. Reach out if you’d like preprints or data summaries.

U–Th Dating of Meteoric Calcite in Altered Fossil Corals

When pristine aragonitic corals are rare, sea-level histories stall. I target the meteoric calcite that fills pores in heavily altered corals and test if it can be dated by U–Th. We map mineral boundaries with LA-ICP-MS and 3D micro-CT, then micro-sample for MC-ICP-MS ages. Early results show usable U (0.06–2.1 ppm) and minimal detrital Th—opening a new path to constrain sea-level change where classic materials are unavailable.

Mid-Holocene ENSO & Monsoon from Coral Trace Elements (Heron Reef)

Two ~60-year coral “snapshots” at ~6.9 and 5.2 ka capture sub-seasonal variability on the southern GBR. High-resolution LA-ICP-MS profiles (e.g., Mg/Ca, Li/Mg, Mn/Ca, REEs) track temperature, river plumes, and water quality. With robust calibration and filtering, we test how ENSO and the Indo-Australian Summer Monsoon varied—and how coastal conditions co-moved—thousands of years before instruments.
