About

My research bridges geochronology, geochemistry, and paleoclimate. I investigate past climate and sea-level variability using coral archives and carbonate systems, with expertise in U–Th geochronology, trace-element geochemistry, and multi-instrument workflows (LA-ICP-MS, MC-ICP-MS, LIBS, micro-CT/3D, SEM). I combine rigorous laboratory methods with Python-based data processing, time-series analysis, and QA/QC for low-level trace-element detection (including REEs).

Read extended bio

I develop high-precision methods for challenging carbonate samples—including meteoric calcite phases in altered fossil corals—to refine sea-level reconstructions. I also build reproducible workflows for data reduction and signal detection (e.g., ENSO) from coral proxies, and mentor students in advanced analytical techniques. Fieldwork includes the Great Barrier Reef and Curaçao; publications span Quaternary Science Reviews, Chemical Geology, and Sedimentary Geology.

Highlighted Work

U–Th Dating of Meteoric Calcite in Fossil Corals

Improving sea-level reconstructions by precisely dating alteration phases in highly altered corals. Methods: micro-sampling, MC-ICP-MS, trace-element screening.

ENSO Signals in Coral LA-ICP-MS Time Series

Seasonality extraction and anomaly detection from Mg/Ca and Li/Mg time series to study ENSO variability with robust signal processing.
